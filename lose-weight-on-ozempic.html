⚡ Quick Answer

On Ozempic, Wegovy or Mounjaro, focus on two numbers above everything else: eating at least 1,000–1,200 kcal/day (never less) and hitting 1.6–2.2g of protein per kg of bodyweight daily. GLP-1 receptor agonists — semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy), tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound) and liraglutide (Saxenda) — are the most effective weight loss medications ever approved. They work by mimicking gut hormones that suppress appetite, slow gastric emptying and reduce cravings. The result: most users naturally eat 40–60% less without feeling deprived.

But this effectiveness creates a hidden problem that standard calorie advice doesn't address.

The GLP-1 Muscle Loss Problem

When you eat very little, very fast, your body doesn't only burn fat — it also breaks down muscle tissue for energy. Research from the landmark SURMOUNT clinical trials on tirzepatide found that in patients who didn't actively prioritise nutrition, up to 40% of total weight lost was lean mass, not fat.

Losing muscle during a GLP-1 cut matters for three reasons: it permanently lowers your TDEE (making future weight maintenance harder), it reduces strength and physical function, and it produces a "soft" body composition rather than the lean physique most people are aiming for.

🚨 The most dangerous mistake on GLP-1 medications

Eating too little. Many patients, empowered by complete appetite suppression, consume only 600–800 kcal/day. This level of restriction causes severe muscle loss, nutritional deficiencies, hair loss and metabolic damage. The minimum safe intake is 1,000 kcal/day for smaller women and 1,200–1,500 kcal/day for men — even if you are not hungry.

Protein: The Most Critical Number on GLP-1s

When eating 1,000–1,400 kcal/day, protein distribution becomes the most important nutritional decision you make. Every gram of protein eaten is a gram that cannot be replaced by muscle tissue.

At 1,200 kcal/day, hitting 2.0g/kg of protein for a 75kg person means eating 150g of protein — which represents 500 kcal just from protein alone, leaving only 700 kcal for fat and carbs. This is why food choices become critical on GLP-1s: ultra-processed foods and empty calories have no place in a 1,200 kcal budget.

Exercise on GLP-1 Medications

Resistance training is not optional on GLP-1 medications — it is the primary defence against muscle loss. Without it, the rapid weight loss driven by the medication disproportionately depletes lean mass.

💊 The resistance training minimum

2–3 sessions per week of resistance training (bodyweight, dumbbells, machines or barbell) combined with adequate protein intake reduces muscle loss during GLP-1 treatment by 60–70% compared to medication alone, based on available recomposition research. Start light — even 30-minute sessions are sufficient.

If low energy from appetite suppression makes gym training feel impossible initially: start with walking (10,000+ steps/day), bodyweight squats, push-ups and resistance bands at home. Any muscle-stimulating activity during the first weeks is better than none.

Common Questions from GLP-1 Users

⚠️ "I'm not hungry at all — do I still need to eat?"

Yes. This is the most important message in this article. Appetite suppression does not mean your body doesn't need nutrition. Going below 1,000–1,200 kcal/day for extended periods causes muscle wasting that does not fully reverse when the medication is stopped. Eat even when not hungry — focus on protein first, then other foods.
